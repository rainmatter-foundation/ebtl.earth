Elephants By The Lake

EBTL is an experiment by the Rainmatter Foundation running over 68 acres of undulating land between a forest and the CT Palli village near Veppanapalli town in Tamil Nadu's Krishnagiri district. The land, home to an old elephant walkway and their waterhole, is surrounded by hills. Many of the valley's slopes and elevations had been flattened by agricultural terraces and bunds.

Once a thriving wetland dotted with small pools of water edged with wild Vetiver (khus) and other perennial grasses... which, over time, had been turned into a largely barren stretch of eroded slopes and mud puddles.

Why?

It is a hopeful attempt to do right by the elements of nature—by the water, by the soil, by the biodiversity—while being sensitive to the needs of the local community. We are attempting this on a patch of Earth that was once a thriving wetland, dotted with small pools of water edged with wild grasses. Our vision is to regenerate the land and its flora and fauna with permaculture, carefully maintaining the balance between the humans who inhabit the surroundings and wildlife that make the land their home. In the end, we hope to create an open playbook that can be replicated elsewhere to restore many more EBTLs.
